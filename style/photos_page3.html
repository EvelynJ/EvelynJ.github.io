Friday 26th December

I woke up this morning to images of my crazy brother preparing for a Boxing Day swim. Well, I too have done a Boxing Day swim, and even a Christmas Day swim. Mine may not have been quite so challenging as Gregs, but it was certainly far warmer and in a nicer environment.

I've included Gregs photo as Anita and Eamonn have this website address as well, and may have missed out on that photo.

Anyway, it's my second night observing, and we have poor weather conditions- there's a lot of thin cloud which means that we can't take an sensitive data. Instead, we are taking a series of images for outreach purposes, which will one day be used to create the pretty pictures you see of galaxies on publicity information. The galaxy below is the first one we have observed (and we are still taking images of it now), and is still the raw image, before any processing to remove artifacts from the CCD, such as dead pixels and the gap between the two CCDs that is clearly visible across the middle of the image. We are taking a series of photos using UV, blue, yellow and red filters, which will be put together to make a pretty, colourful image of the galaxy. The reason we use so many different filters is because the CCDs can only detect light, not colour, so all images are in black and white. The filters are also useful for showing different information about the target. For example, young stars and star-forming regions are very blue, so show up brightly in the blue filter but are almost invisible in the red filters. In turn, the red filters show up the old stars better, as they shine red, and the intermediate age stars shine brightest in the yellow light. By putting the different images in different filters together we can get a pretty image that shows all this information. Wednesday 24th December

It's always interesting to fly on Christmas Eve to see what people are trying to take with them. Here in Chile, the local airlines aren't stingy about baggage allowance, allowing you two suitcases up to 23kg plus two items of hand baggage. People are really making the most of that allowance here, taking massive bags of christmas presents home with them. I even saw someone with a box for a freezer on a trolly. I have no idea what was in the box, but it looked unopened. Anyway, to get you into the festive mood, at the airport and on the plane they are giving away lots of free goodies.
